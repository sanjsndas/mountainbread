Terms of Privacy

Your data, our covenant · last updated April 2026

Our commitment

Mountain Bread Chronicle respects your privacy. We collect only the minimum data needed to deliver our content and improve your experience. We never sell, rent, or trade your personal information.

This policy explains what data we gather, how we use it, and your rights. By using our site, you agree to the terms outlined below.

📌 GDPR compliant 🔐 encrypted

What we collect

  • Anonymous analytics: page views, referral sources, browser type (via self-hosted analytics).
  • Comments & feedback: if you post, we store your name, email, and IP address (to prevent spam).
  • Newsletter signups: email address and name (if provided) — we use a double‑opt‑in process.
  • Cookies: minimal functional cookies for site preferences and comment sessions. See our Cookie Policy.

🛡️ Your rights

You have the right to access, correct, or delete your data at any time. Contact us at privacy@mountainbread.ch and we will respond within 30 days.

We also offer data portability and the right to object to processing.

Data security

All data is stored on secure servers with encryption at rest and in transit. We regularly review our systems to protect against unauthorized access. However, no internet transmission is 100% secure — we cannot guarantee absolute security, but we strive to use commercially acceptable means.

Third‑party services

We use minimal third‑party tools: a privacy‑focused analytics provider and an email service for newsletters. These processors are GDPR‑compliant and bound by data protection agreements. We do not use advertising networks or trackers.
