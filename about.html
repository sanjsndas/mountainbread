About this chronicle

Field notes from the grain belt · since 2023

Our mission

Mountain Bread Chronicle is a living archive of grain, dough, and the human stories behind them. We bridge ancient traditions with modern baking science, exploring terroir, fermentation, and the cultural memory embedded in every loaf.

We believe bread is not merely food — it is a text, a ritual, a quiet revolution. Our editorial compass points toward depth over speed, heritage over hype, and craft over convenience.

🌱 since 2023 📖 200+ articles

Editorial principles

  • Curiosity: We ask hard questions about what we eat and why.
  • Integrity: No sponsored content — every recommendation is earned.
  • Community: We amplify voices from millers, bakers, and grain growers.
  • Ecology: We champion biodiversity and soil health through grain diversity.

How we work

Our contributors are bakers, archaeologists, agronomists, and storytellers. Each article undergoes a rigorous review — not for orthodoxy, but for clarity, curiosity, and respect for the grain. We travel to mills, attend heritage wheat festivals, and maintain a living library of sourdough starters from around the world.

We are entirely reader-supported. If you value what we do, consider sharing our work or joining our newsletter (coming soon).
